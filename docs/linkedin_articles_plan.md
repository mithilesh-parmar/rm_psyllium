## 2. How LinkedIn articles work for ranking (short version)

- Write **articles**, not posts. A post lives in the feed for a day. An article gets its own web page that Google can find.
- **Use LinkedIn SEO settings.** Put the words people search at the front of the title, then use LinkedIn's article settings for the Article URL, SEO title, and SEO description.
- **Length helps.** Aim for 900–1,400 words. Thin articles don't rank.
- **One link back to your site**, placed where it makes sense. Not five.
- **After you publish, share it as a normal post** the same day with two lines and the link in the first comment (not in the post itself — LinkedIn shows posts less when there's an outside link in the body).
- **Be patient and honest about it.** A new profile won't rank for "psyllium supplier" soon. It can rank for the specific questions buyers type, which is where the orders come from anyway.
