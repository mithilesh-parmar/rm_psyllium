#### 📝 Post 2: The Science of Swelling Volume
*   **Target Audience**: QA Managers, Phytomedicine Procurement Leads
*   **Key Specs Used**: Swelling volume >40 ml/g, USP/EP methodology

---

**Copy & Paste:**

Is your psyllium supplier providing a generic Certificate of Analysis (COA)?

If your QA team is only looking at the purity percentage, you are missing the ultimate indicator of functional quality: Swelling Volume.

The swelling volume is the measure of how much water the psyllium mucilage can absorb and retain. Under United States Pharmacopeia (USP) and European Pharmacopoeia (EP) methodologies:
- High-grade 99% purity psyllium husk must achieve a swelling volume exceeding 40 ml/g.
- Lower grades (e.g., 85% food-grade) typically sit below 30 ml/g.

Why does this number drop?
1. Seed Coat Contamination: If the seed cleaning process leaves heavy parts of the seed body or embryo (which have no swelling capacity), the swelling volume drops.
2. Adulteration: Unscrupulous traders blending low-viscosity plant-byproducts (khakha powder) into high-purity lots.
3. Excessive Heat during Milling: High temperatures during mechanical milling degrade the high-molecular-weight polysaccharides in the husk mucilage, destroying its water-binding properties.

#### 📝 Post 3: The Chemistry of Ash & Moisture Control
*   **Target Audience**: Food Safety Managers, Clean-Label Food Brands
*   **Key Specs Used**: Moisture < 10%, Total Ash < 4%

---

**Copy & Paste:**

In B2B psyllium sourcing, two parameters dictate the shelf life of your product: Moisture and Ash.

Let's break down the chemistry:

1. Moisture Content (Strictly < 10%):
Psyllium husk is highly hygroscopic. If your supplier ships a lot with 11% or 12% moisture to save on drying costs, you are buying a microbiological risk. High moisture in sealed B2B bags during transit (especially across warm shipping routes) creates a perfect breeding ground for mold, yeast, and Salmonella.

2. Total Ash Content (Strictly < 4% for Food Grade, < 3% for USP):
Ash represents the inorganic mineral residue left after burning the sample. In psyllium, high ash content is a direct indicator of soil, sand, and agricultural dust contamination. If the cleaning phase of the seed is rushed, soil particles stick to the husk, inflating the ash percentage.

When you buy cheap psyllium, you are often paying for moisture and sand.

At RM Psyllium, we utilize advanced dry-air processing and multi-stage air classifiers to keep moisture consistently under 10% and total ash under the 4% limit. We don't hide behind "representative certificates"—your specific lot gets its own NABL lab analysis.

Clean formulations require clean raw materials.
